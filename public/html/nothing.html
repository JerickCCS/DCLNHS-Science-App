Part Description and Function(s)
Mechanical
Draw tube A small cylinder attached to the upper part of the body tube that holds the ocular lens.
Body tube Connects the ocular lens to the revolving nosepiece.
Coarse adjustment knob Moves the body tube or stage upward or downward in larger increments to bring the specimen into initial focus; used only with the scanner or low power objective.
Fine adjustment knob Moves the body tube or stage upward or downward in smaller increments to bring the specimen into sharp focus; preferably used with the high power or oil immersion objective.
Arm Supports the body tube and is used to carry the microscope.
Revolving nosepiece A circular rotating part connected to the body tube that holds the objectives.
Stage A platform where the specimen slide is placed; has a hole at the center for light to pass through.
Stage clips Secure the specimen slide on the stage.
Inclination joint Attaches the arm to the pillar and allows tilting of the microscope.
Pillar Provides support above the base and where the arm is attached.
Base Provides firm and steady support to the entire microscope.
Magnifying
Ocular lens or eyepiece A detachable cylinder on top of the draw tube used to view the specimen; usually magnifies up to 10x and may contain a pointer.
Objective lens Magnifies the specimen. Typically includes scanner (if present), low power (LPO), high power (HPO), and oil immersion (OIO) objectives. Used in that order when focusing.
Illuminating
Condenser Concentrates light onto the specimen; located above the iris diaphragm and may have adjustment knobs.
Iris diaphragm Regulates the amount of light passing through the specimen; controlled by a movable lever.
Mirror Reflects light to the specimen slide and back to the eyes; located below the stage near the base.
